01 / The Governance Gap

India's Invisible Policy-Delivery Chasm

India has built world-class digital infrastructure — DBT, Aadhaar, PFMS, PM-KISAN portals. Yet a profound gap persists between policy design and citizen-experienced reality.

🏗️

Scheme Infrastructure Exists

India has over 50 centrally sponsored schemes, robust DBT frameworks, and Aadhaar-linked delivery pipelines. On paper, the architecture is world-class.

🌫️

Citizens Remain Excluded

Millions of eligible citizens — tenant farmers, migrants, informal workers — are systematically excluded due to documentation gaps, procedural opacity, and informational asymmetry.

🔇

Failures Go Undetected

When schemes fail at last-mile, there is no structured feedback mechanism. Local failures never surface as national intelligence. Policy design never learns from citizen experience.

⚖️

Legal Remedy Is Inaccessible

Rights exist on paper — RTI, grievance redressal, legal notice frameworks are available — but most citizens lack the knowledge, language, or process support to use them effectively.

Current systems are fragmented across silos: scheme portals hold eligibility data, legal databases hold remedy frameworks, reports hold failure analysis — but no unified layer connects policy → citizen → issue → action → correction.

MGNREGA Intelligence

MGNREGA guarantees 100 days of wage employment per rural household. BRT activates the legally enforceable rights that most citizens don't know they possess.

  • Job demand not acknowledged within 15-day legal window
  • Wage delays beyond 15-day payment mandate
  • Work allocation in different villages violating local preference rights
  • Unemployment allowance not paid when work isn't provided
  • Muster roll manipulation at gram panchayat level
  • Women's guaranteed 1/3 participation right being violated

07 / Trust Architecture

Built for Public Trust

A platform that handles citizen rights and governance intelligence must be held to the highest standards of transparency, safety, and ethics.

🧑‍⚖️

Human-in-the-Loop

All legal document generation requires citizen review and consent before submission. No automated filing without explicit human confirmation.

🔍

Transparent Logic

Every eligibility determination and legal pathway is explained in plain language. Citizens understand why they received a specific recommendation.

🔒

Consent-Based Data

No citizen data is stored without explicit opt-in. Intelligence signals are anonymised at source. Individual identities are never part of governance datasets.

📊

Non-Punitive Analytics

Governance intelligence is used for policy improvement, not for profiling or penalising citizens, officers, or communities.

⚖️

Strong Grievance Access

Citizens can challenge any BRT output, flag incorrect recommendations, and access a transparent dispute pathway for platform decisions.

🌐

Open Architecture

Core intelligence layers will be made available to civil society organisations, parliamentary researchers, and journalists for accountability purposes.
